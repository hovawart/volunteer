About Porchlight

Built on the idea that being heard is its own kind of help.

Porchlight started as a small group of neighbors who noticed how often people just needed to talk — not to be treated, not to be given a five-step plan, just heard. We built a simple way to match those people with volunteers willing to listen well.

Our story

How Porchlight came together

Year one

A handful of conversations

A few friends started informally offering their time to anyone who wanted to talk. Word spread faster than expected — the need was clearly there.

Year two

A real intake process

We built a lightweight screening and training process for volunteers, and set clear guidelines about what the program would and wouldn't do.

Today

A standing volunteer network

Porchlight now runs as an ongoing community project, matching people who want to talk with volunteers who've been trained to listen with care and stay within their role.

What we believe

The values behind the program

Listening is the whole job

Volunteers aren't there to fix, advise, or diagnose. They're there to give someone their full attention.

Boundaries keep it safe

Clear limits protect both the person calling and the volunteer, and they're non-negotiable — see below.

No pressure, no clock

Conversations move at the visitor's pace. There's no script to get through and no outcome to hit.

Safety & boundaries

What every volunteer commits to

Screened before they start

Every volunteer completes an application, a short interview, and reference checks before taking a first session.

Trained in active listening

Volunteers complete guided training on listening skills, staying in their lane, and recognizing when someone needs more support than Porchlight can give.

Never diagnose or advise

Volunteers are not clinicians. They don't offer medical, legal, or psychological advice, and they're trained to redirect to professionals when that's what's needed.

Point toward crisis care fast

If a conversation touches on immediate danger, volunteers pause and point directly to crisis lines and emergency services — every time, no exceptions.

Keep it confidential

What's said in a session stays there, aside from the rare safety exceptions volunteers are trained to recognize and disclose upfront.

Supported, not alone

Volunteers have access to a coordinator and peer supervision, so they're never carrying a hard conversation by themselves either.
